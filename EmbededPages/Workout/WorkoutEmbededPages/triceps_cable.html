Panatta Triceps Extension

The Panatta triceps extension is an isolation exercise that targets the triceps muscles, helping to increase strength and definition in the back of the arms.

1Adjust the seat and handles of the Panatta triceps machine to align with your body height and range of motion.

2Sit on the machine with your back against the backrest and feet flat on the floor.

3Grasp the handles with an overhand grip, keeping your elbows close to your sides and bent at 90 degrees.

4Extend your elbows and push the handles downward until your arms are fully extended, focusing on contracting your triceps muscles.

5Slowly return to the starting position, maintaining control throughout the movement and keeping tension on the triceps.

6Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Triceps Pushdown

The triceps pushdown is a classic exercise that targets the triceps muscles, helping to build strength and definition in the back of the arms.

1Attach a straight or angled bar to the high pulley of a cable machine.

2Stand facing the machine with feet shoulder-width apart and grasp the bar with an overhand grip.

3Keep your elbows close to your sides and fully extend your arms downward, focusing on squeezing your triceps at the bottom of the movement.

4Slowly return to the starting position, controlling the weight and maintaining tension on the triceps throughout the exercise.

5Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Overhead Triceps Extension

The overhead triceps extension targets the long head of the triceps, helping to improve strength and muscle definition in the back of the arms.

1Attach a rope or a dumbbell handle to the high pulley of a cable machine.

2Stand facing away from the machine and grasp the rope or handle with both hands, palms facing upward.

3Extend your arms overhead, keeping your elbows close to your head and your upper arms stationary.

4Lower the rope or handle behind your head by bending your elbows, then extend your arms to return to the starting position.

5Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Triceps Rope Pushdown

The triceps rope pushdown is a variation of the classic pushdown exercise, targeting the triceps muscles from a different angle.

1Attach a rope handle to the high pulley of a cable machine.

2Stand facing the machine with feet shoulder-width apart and grasp the rope with an overhand grip.

3Keep your elbows close to your sides and extend your arms downward, focusing on squeezing your triceps at the bottom of the movement.

4Slowly return to the starting position, controlling the weight and maintaining tension on the triceps throughout the exercise.

5Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Triceps Dips

Triceps dips are a bodyweight exercise that targets the triceps muscles, helping to improve strength and muscle definition in the back of the arms.

1Position yourself between parallel bars or use a dip machine, gripping the bars with your hands shoulder-width apart.

2Straighten your arms and lift your body off the ground or platform, keeping your elbows slightly bent.

3Lower your body by bending your elbows until they reach a 90-degree angle, then push yourself back up to the starting position.

4Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, focusing on using your triceps muscles to lift and lower your body.
