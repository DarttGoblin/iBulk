Dumbbell Bench Press

The dumbbell bench press is a classic exercise for building chest strength and muscle mass. It targets the pectoralis major, anterior deltoids, and triceps.

1Lie flat on a bench with a dumbbell in each hand, held at chest level.

2Push the dumbbells upward until your arms are fully extended, then lower them back down to chest level.

3Keep your back flat on the bench and maintain control throughout the movement.

4Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Incline Dumbbell Press

The incline dumbbell press targets the upper portion of the chest, helping to develop a well-rounded chest appearance.

1Set an incline bench to a 30-45 degree angle.

2Lie back on the bench with a dumbbell in each hand, arms extended upward directly above your chest.

3Lower the dumbbells down to the sides of your chest, keeping your elbows at a 45-degree angle.

4Press the dumbbells back up to the starting position, fully extending your arms.

5Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Dumbbell Flyes

Dumbbell flyes target the outer and inner regions of the chest, helping to increase chest width and definition.

1Lie flat on a bench with a dumbbell in each hand, arms extended upward directly above your chest.

2With a slight bend in your elbows, lower the dumbbells out to the sides in a wide arc until you feel a stretch in your chest.

3Raise the dumbbells back to the starting position using a controlled motion.

4Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
