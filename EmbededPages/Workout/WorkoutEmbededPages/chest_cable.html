Machine Chest Press

The machine chest press is a great exercise for beginners or those looking for a stable and controlled movement to target the chest muscles.

1Adjust the seat and handles of the chest press machine to align with your chest height and grip preference.

2Sit on the machine with your back flat against the backrest and feet planted firmly on the floor.

3Grasp the handles with an overhand grip, keeping your elbows bent at 90 degrees and in line with your shoulders.

4Push the handles forward until your arms are fully extended, focusing on squeezing your chest muscles at the top of the movement.

5Slowly bring the handles back to the starting position under control, maintaining tension in the chest muscles.

6Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Cable Chest Press

The cable chest press is an effective exercise for targeting the chest muscles while providing constant tension throughout the movement.

1Set up cables to chest height on a cable machine and grasp the handles with an overhand grip.

2Stand with one foot forward and one foot back, maintaining a slight bend in the elbows.

3Push the handles forward and together until your arms are fully extended, keeping your chest up and core engaged.

4Slowly reverse the movement, bringing the handles back to the starting position under control.

5Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Cable Crossover

The cable crossover is an excellent exercise for targeting the inner and outer chest muscles, providing a full range of motion and stretch.

1Set the cables to the highest position on a cable machine and select the appropriate weight.

2Stand in the center of the machine with feet shoulder-width apart and grasp the handles with an overhand grip.

3Step forward slightly, keeping a slight bend in the elbows, and lean forward from the hips.

4Pull the handles downward and across your body in a sweeping motion until your hands meet in front of your hips.

5Slowly reverse the movement, returning to the starting position under control.

6Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Cable Chest Flyes

Cable chest flyes target the chest muscles, providing a great stretch and contraction for chest development.

1Adjust the pulleys on a cable machine to shoulder height and select the appropriate weight.

2Stand between the pulleys and hold one handle in each hand with palms facing inward.

3Step forward with one foot for stability and slightly lean forward from the hips.

4Keep a slight bend in the elbows and bring your arms forward and together in front of you, crossing slightly at the top.

5Slowly reverse the movement, opening your arms wide until you feel a stretch in your chest.

6Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Single Arm Cable Chest Press

The single arm cable chest press allows for unilateral training, helping to address strength imbalances between the left and right sides of the chest.

1Set up a single handle attachment at chest height on a cable machine and select the appropriate weight.

2Stand perpendicular to the machine and grasp the handle with one hand, stepping away to create tension in the cable.

3Maintain a strong stance with feet shoulder-width apart and engage your core for stability.

4Push the handle forward and away from your body until your arm is fully extended, keeping your chest up.

5Slowly bring the handle back to the starting position under control, maintaining tension in the chest.

6Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch sides.

Low to High Cable Chest Flyes

Low to high cable chest flyes emphasize the upper chest muscles, helping to develop a well-rounded chest appearance.

1Set the pulleys on a cable machine to the lowest position and select the appropriate weight.

2Stand facing away from the machine with feet shoulder-width apart and grasp the handles with an overhand grip.

3Step forward slightly to create tension in the cables and lean forward from the hips.

4Keep a slight bend in the elbows and bring your arms up and together in front of you, crossing slightly at the top.

5Slowly reverse the movement, opening your arms wide until you feel a stretch in your upper chest.

6Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
